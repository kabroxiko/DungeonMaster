Initial adventure seed:
Produce a richer, multi-paragraph opening scene (3–5 short paragraphs) rather than a single brief paragraph. The opening should still be narrative-first and avoid mechanical details.
Structure the output as follows:
  1) Brief context (1–2 sentences): who the character is (use provided character data when available), where they are, and why they are here right now. IMPORTANT: Begin with a mundane, unrelated reason the character is present (e.g., resting, traveling, working, visiting), not an explicit mission.

  3) Immediate hook and stakes (1 short paragraph): the narrative should introduce an unexpected element (a clue, an odd NPC behavior, a sign of a faction, or an environmental anomaly) that naturally pulls the character toward a larger concern tied to the campaign background — do NOT start with a predefined quest or directive.
  4) Leads & threads (1 short paragraph): mention existing threads, clues, or relevant NPCs neutrally. Do NOT frame these as suggested actions, recommended approaches, or explicit options for the player.
- Do NOT include or render the character sheet in your response; the server will display the Character Sheet separately on the client UI.
- IMPORTANT: Do NOT mention combat mechanics, initiative lists, HP trackers, or other encounter formatting in the narrative opening. Do NOT present explicit numbered options or A/B/C menus. Keep output immersive and usable for play.
Style guidance: keep voice immersive, sensory, and player-focused. Avoid prefatory meta-text such as "Here's how it might look" or any explanations about the prompt. Produce only the narrative opening content when asked.
